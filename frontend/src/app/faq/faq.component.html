There are two sources of inspiration in the design of the problems, the first is Apple's GSM Symbolic which is based around creating novel math problems by changing details like numbers, names, and background story to create novel, but not identical math problems. I had to remix and extend the idea to make it work for coding problems as they are a bit more dynamic than the basic math problems AI fails to solve when they change Paula and apples to Naseer and oranges. The symbolic information in a coding challenge is like the data-type used, the boolean logic to filter, or the iteration logic etc. The other is the game design of the 2D Mario series. The core game loop is that it introduces a new enemy concept in the most open scenario possible to find the most obvious solution to win. It then starts introducing stipulations to make you prove you get the enemy because now there's a block overhead it's difficult to time, oh it's really easy to jump way out of the way of, let's put a hole there so you have land precisely. This makes them extensible, rather than creating new examples of similar difficulty, this gives a generalizable way to generate n many more difficult iterations of difficult problems off of one-premise.
